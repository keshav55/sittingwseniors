Our Story

The mission of Sitting with Seniors is to inspire stronger cross-generational relationships between young adults and senior citizens through leisurely activities.

In July of 2012, my grandfather entered a nursing home. After his double bypass heart surgery, he was well enough that did not need the intensive care afforded to him at Washington Hospital but not so well that we could properly care for and monitor him at home. So, for a period of three weeks, he stayed at Park Central Care and Rehabilitation Center. Just about every day, I would go and spend time with and play cards and Dominos (his favorite game). Though a nurse came by every so often, my grandfather Charanjit (he always wanted people to call him CJ) was lonely and needed me there to keep his company.

Seeing us playing cards and Dominos, a few other seniors who were mobile began to join us at the table. Eventually, at just about any given time of the day, there were three Dominos games going and about one or two games of Rummy. Eventually, I began bringing two of my friends as well and soon enough, we had established a way for some of the seniors to feel the company of someone from “the outside” (as my grandfather described it).

In February of 2013, my grandfather passed away. The onset of dementia, combined with his ongoing heart problems, proved too much for him in the end. To honor his memory, I spent nearly every weekend volunteering at nursing homes and senior centers through American Home Health Care. For three years, I did just about everything with seniors: playing cards, exchanging stories, getting girl advice, helping them set up email and Facebook accounts, and even catching a few (Bollywood) movies.

By June of 2013, I felt that I had the experience and skill to expand the program and organize young people to give company to many seniors. At this point, I recruited a core group of volunteers, thereby founding Sitting with Seniors.

To apply as a volunteer, you must be:

  • Passionate about working with seniors
  • Fluent in 2 or more languages
  • CPR and First-Aid Certified
  • Attach A resume or CV
  • Be willing to take part in a 10-15 minute interview
  • Must be willing to volunteer at least 2 hours a month
